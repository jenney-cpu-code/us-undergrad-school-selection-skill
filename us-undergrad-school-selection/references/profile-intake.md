# Applicant Intake Checklist

Use this checklist to gather only the inputs that materially affect school-list quality.

## Critical Inputs
- citizenship and current country of residence
- current grade and expected entry year
- school curriculum: U.S. high school, AP, IB, A-Level, Canadian, local curriculum, etc.
- transcript strength: GPA, class rank, school rigor, and any context about grade inflation or deflation
- testing: SAT or ACT, English proficiency, AP or IB or A-Level scores, predicted scores if relevant
- intended major, alternate major, and whether the student is open to undecided admission
- annual family budget in USD and whether financial aid is required
- preference for research university versus liberal arts college
- preferred regions, climate, campus setting, school size, and safety expectations
- family priorities: prestige, employment outcomes, flexibility, merit aid, support for international students, Chinese or international community, etc.

## High-Value Secondary Inputs
- top extracurricular themes and awards
- research, internship, entrepreneurship, or artistic profile
- athletics, legacy, first-generation, or other hooks if applicable
- ED or REA openness
- tolerance for religious affiliation, rural setting, cold weather, Greek life, or core curriculum
- preferred distance from major airports or large cities

## Minimum Follow-Up Questions
If the user gives only a rough profile, ask for the smallest set of clarifications that changes recommendations the most:
1. What is the student's intended major, and how fixed is it?
2. What is the maximum annual budget in USD?
3. Does the student need financial aid or merit scholarships to attend?
4. What are the strongest academic indicators available now?
5. Are there hard constraints on region, size, or campus type?

## Assumption Mode
If the user wants a fast first draft, proceed with explicit assumptions. State them near the top of the answer and note which assumptions most affect the final list.
