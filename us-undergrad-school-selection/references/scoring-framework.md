# Scoring Framework

Use a weighted framework so recommendations are transparent and easy to adjust.

## Default Weighting
- 30 points: admission competitiveness
- 25 points: affordability and aid realism
- 20 points: academic and major fit
- 15 points: outcomes and support environment
- 10 points: preference fit

## When to Reweight
- Tight budget: increase affordability to 30-35 points
- Highly selective major: increase academic and major fit to 25-30 points
- Prestige-driven family with flexible budget: keep admission competitiveness high, but do not let prestige override hard constraints
- Student with strong location preferences: increase preference fit enough to remove unrealistic options

## Dimension Checks

### Admission Competitiveness
Check:
- how the student's grades, rigor, testing, and profile compare with CDS ranges and school priorities
- whether the student benefits from ED or EA options
- whether international status, aid need, or major choice makes the school effectively harder than the raw admit rate suggests

### Affordability and Aid Realism
Check:
- published cost of attendance
- eligibility for international need-based aid
- eligibility for merit scholarships and whether they are competitive or automatic
- likelihood that the school can land near the family budget

Separate `high sticker price but possible with aid` from `financially unrealistic`.

### Academic and Major Fit
Check:
- strength and accessibility of the intended major
- direct-admit, capped, or portfolio barriers
- ease of switching majors
- research, internship, honors, or advising opportunities relevant to the student

### Outcomes and Support Environment
Check:
- retention and graduation indicators
- class size and faculty access
- support for international students, writing support, internship access, and location advantages

### Preference Fit
Check:
- size, setting, region, climate, culture, and campus feel
- social environment, housing expectations, and student-life preferences

## Tiering Guidance
Use tiers as strategy labels, not promises.

- `Lottery reach`: admission is possible but highly uncertain even for excellent applicants
- `Reach`: plausible but clearly uphill because of selectivity, major access, aid, or international competition
- `Target`: credible fit with some favorable data points, but still not assured
- `Possible likely`: stronger fit with fewer obvious barriers, though still verify finance and major access
- `Financial anchor`: included because cost may be more realistic through merit or lower baseline cost

Avoid calling a school a true safety when all three conditions are not met:
- admission looks favorable
- cost is realistically manageable
- intended major access is not blocked
