# CDS and Official Data Checklist

## Source Priority
1. Latest official CDS PDF or official CDS webpage from the university
2. Official admissions pages
3. Official international admissions pages
4. Official financial-aid and scholarship pages
5. Official academic department or registrar pages
6. Institutional research or facts-and-figures pages

Do not use third-party summaries when an official source covers the point.

## What to Extract from CDS

### Section A: General Information
Use for school control, setting, calendar system, and high-level institutional context.

### Section B: Enrollment and Persistence
Use for undergraduate size, retention, and graduation context. These help with environment and student outcomes.

### Section C: First-Time, First-Year Admission
This is usually the most important section for school-list work.

Extract when available:
- number of applicants, admits, and enrolled students
- overall admit rate
- test policy and score reporting context
- middle 50 percent or other score distributions for SAT and ACT
- percentage of freshmen in top tenth or top quarter of class
- secondary school GPA or class rank data
- level of applicant interest in rigor, GPA, tests, essays, recommendations, extracurriculars, talent, character, and other admission factors
- waitlist data if it materially affects strategy

### Section D: Transfer Admission
Usually low priority for first-year applicants. Ignore unless the user explicitly asks about transfer pathways.

### Section E: Academic Offerings and Policies
Use to check program structure, degree offerings, and academic flexibility.

### Section F: Student Life
Use selectively for housing, campus living expectations, and environment fit.

### Section G: Annual Expenses
Extract tuition, fees, room, board, books, and estimated total cost of attendance.

### Section H: Financial Aid
Extract when available:
- number of students with need
- percentage of need met
- average need-based award
- average non-need-based award
- self-help or loan expectations if stated

Important: CDS financial-aid tables often describe enrolled students overall, not international-aid policy. Always supplement with official international-aid pages.

### Section I: Instructional Faculty and Class Size
Use for classroom experience, student-faculty ratio, and small-class access.

### Section J: Degrees Conferred
Use as a rough signal for academic breadth and where the school graduates many students.

## Data CDS Often Does Not Fully Cover
Always verify these from official school pages:
- whether the school is need-blind or need-aware for international students
- whether internationals can receive need-based aid, merit aid, or both
- required aid forms for internationals such as CSS Profile or ISFAA
- current testing policy year
- English proficiency requirements and waiver rules
- direct-admit, capped, or portfolio-based majors
- special application plans such as ED, REA, EA, rolling admission, or priority scholarship deadlines

## Citation Standard
For each school, keep notes in a compact format such as:
- `School Name - CDS 2024-2025 Section C9`
- `School Name - official international admissions page, accessed 2026-05-04`
- `School Name - official financial aid page for international students, accessed 2026-05-04`

If you cannot verify a claim from an official source, say that the point is unverified.
